# Soil Health & Irrigation Management

## Soil pH Guidelines
- **Ideal for most crops**: 6.0–7.5.
- **Acidic soil (pH < 6)**: common in Kerala, NE, hills, W Bengal. Add **agricultural lime** (CaCO3) 200–500 kg/acre depending on severity; or use crushed limestone / dolomite (adds Mg).
- **Alkaline / saline / sodic soil (pH > 8)**: common in parts of Punjab, Haryana, UP, Rajasthan. Add **gypsum** (CaSO4·2H2O) 200–500 kg/acre + adequate irrigation to leach salts.
- Get pH tested FREE via Soil Health Card — no guessing.

## NPK Status Interpretation (Soil Health Card)
- **Nitrogen (N)**:
  - Low (< 280 kg/ha) → increase dose 25%
  - Medium (280–560) → standard dose
  - High (> 560) → reduce by 25%
- **Phosphorus (P)**:
  - Low (< 10 kg P2O5/ha) → increase 25%
  - Medium (10–25) → standard
  - High (> 25) → reduce 25%
- **Potassium (K)**:
  - Low (< 120 kg K2O/ha) → increase 25%
  - Medium (120–280) → standard
  - High (> 280) → reduce 25%

## Organic Carbon (OC) — Most important metric
- Low (< 0.5%) = degraded soil → URGENT action: add FYM 8–10 t/acre, grow green manure (dhaincha, sunhemp), mulch all residues.
- Medium (0.5–0.75%) → maintain with 5 t FYM/acre/year.
- High (> 0.75%) → keep up practices.

## Micronutrient Deficiencies — Common in India
- **Zinc (Zn)**: widespread — apply ZnSO4 10 kg/acre at sowing, especially for rice, wheat, maize.
- **Iron (Fe)**: in calcareous soils — foliar spray FeSO4 0.5% (5 g/L) + citric acid 1 g/L.
- **Sulphur (S)**: needed by oilseeds, pulses — apply gypsum 100 kg/acre OR elemental S 10 kg/acre.
- **Boron (B)**: for oilseeds, vegetables, fruits — borax 4 kg/acre OR foliar 0.2% (2 g/L) at flowering.
- **Manganese (Mn)**: foliar MnSO4 0.5% if deficiency signs.

## Irrigation — Golden Rules
- **Sandy soil**: small frequent irrigations (every 4–5 days, shallow depth).
- **Clay soil**: deep but infrequent (every 8–12 days).
- **Loamy (best)**: moderate frequency.
- **Never irrigate at noon** in summer — high evaporation + leaf scald.
- **Early morning** best (cool + pest/disease less).
- **Field capacity to 50% depletion** rule — irrigate before soil moisture drops below 50%.

## Micro-Irrigation (recommended)
- **Drip**: saves 40–60% water vs flood; perfect for row crops, orchards, vegetables. Subsidy via PMKSY (55% small/marginal, 45% others).
- **Sprinkler**: saves 30–50%; good for wheat, pulses, vegetables.
- **Mulching** (plastic or organic) with drip: saves another 20% water, suppresses weeds, moderates soil temperature.

## Water Budget (per crop, total requirement)
- Paddy: 1200–1500 mm
- Wheat: 400–500 mm
- Cotton (Bt, irrigated): 700–1000 mm
- Sugarcane: 1800–2500 mm
- Maize: 500–700 mm
- Tomato / vegetables: 400–600 mm
- Groundnut: 500–600 mm
- Pulses: 300–400 mm (drought-tolerant)

## Water-Saving Practices
- **Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD)** for paddy: saves 25–30% water, no yield loss.
- **System of Rice Intensification (SRI)**: wider spacing (25×25), young seedlings, intermittent irrigation — 20% more yield with 40% less water.
- **Direct Seeded Rice (DSR)**: skip transplanting; saves ~30% water + labour.
- **Laser land leveling**: uniform water spread, saves 25% water.
- **Mulching**: 5–10 cm straw/plastic cover, saves 20% irrigation.
- **Rainwater harvesting**: farm ponds (subsidy under MGNREGA + state schemes like Karnataka's Krishi Bhagya — 90% for SC/ST/small).

## Fertilizer Application Principles
- **4R principle**: Right Source, Right Rate, Right Time, Right Place.
- **Split application** of urea (N): 40% basal, 30% at vegetative, 30% at flowering — reduces leaching loss.
- **Banding / placement** (side-dressing) near root zone: 20–30% less fertilizer needed vs broadcasting.
- **Urea + neem coating**: slows nitrification, saves 10–15% N.
- **DAP + MOP** at sowing (basal) — most crops.
- **Foliar sprays**: useful for micronutrients, late-stage N, and quick corrections. 2% urea (20 g/L), 1% KNO3 (10 g/L), 0.5% ZnSO4 (5 g/L).

## Soil Testing — When & How
- **When**: before new crop cycle, ideally every 2 years.
- **How to sample**: collect 10–15 spots in zig-zag pattern across field, 15 cm depth, mix thoroughly, take 500 g, dry in shade, submit to KVK / block lab with Soil Health Card form.
- **Read**: your Soil Health Card tells you your soil's own fertilizer recipe — use it, don't follow generic dose blindly.

## Green Manuring
- Before main Kharif, sow a fast-growing legume:
  - **Dhaincha (Sesbania)**: 20 kg seed/acre, grows in 45–50 days, plough in at flowering → 40–60 kg N/acre.
  - **Sunhemp (Crotalaria)**: 25 kg/acre, same cycle.
  - **Cowpea**: dual-purpose (pods + green manure).
- Benefits: adds 60+ kg N/ha, improves organic carbon, breaks pest cycle.

## Crop Rotation — Smart Sequences
- **Rice – wheat** (Punjab, Haryana, UP) — but causes groundwater depletion; diversify with maize/pulses every 3rd year.
- **Rice – pulse (chana/masoor)** — fixes N, saves water.
- **Cotton – wheat / gram** — wheat captures residual fertility.
- **Soybean – wheat** — popular MP / Maharashtra.
- **Tobacco – groundnut** (South).
- **Include pulses** at least every 2–3 years — they fix atmospheric N and break pest cycles.

## Drainage — Underrated
- **Waterlogged fields** lose 30–50% yield. Ensure field has slight slope (0.5%) and open drains.
- In clay / flood-prone areas: raised-bed + furrow system.
- In saline/alkaline areas: sub-surface drainage (if affordable) + gypsum flush.

## Zero Budget Natural Farming (ZBNF) — basics
- **Jeevamrut**: 10 kg cow dung + 10 L cow urine + 2 kg jaggery + 2 kg pulse flour + 200 L water → ferment 48 hr → apply 200 L/acre/fortnight.
- **Beejamrut**: seed treatment with cow-dung-urine-lime mix.
- **Mulching** (soil always covered).
- **Intercropping / multi-cropping** (never monoculture).
- Andhra Pradesh & parts of Karnataka state-promoted.
