# Organic / Natural Farming & Market Intelligence

## Organic Farming Basics
- Certified organic = 3-year transition period, mandatory record-keeping, certified agency audit.
- PGS-India (Participatory Guarantee System): peer-based certification for small farmers — cheaper, faster.
- NPOP (National Programme for Organic Production): for export-grade certification.
- **Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER)**: subsidies for NE organic growers.

## Natural Inputs (Farmer-Made)
- **Jeevamrut** (fermented cow-dung + urine + jaggery + pulse flour): biofertiliser + soil microbiome booster. Apply 200 L/acre/fortnight via irrigation water.
- **Panchagavya**: 5 cow-products mix (dung + urine + ghee + milk + curd) + fruit pulps, fermented 21 days. Foliar 3% (30 ml/L).
- **Beejamrut**: seed treatment — cow dung 5 kg + cow urine 5 L + lime 50 g + handful soil + 20 L water. Coat seeds, dry, sow.
- **Agni-astra**: neem leaves + garlic + green chilli + tobacco, boiled in cow urine. Pest repellent — 5 ml/L foliar.
- **Brahmastra, Neemastra**: neem-leaf extracts for pest control.

## Biofertilisers (available at KVKs / agri dealers)
- **Rhizobium**: for pulses (chana, tur, urad, moong, soybean, groundnut). 200 g / 10 kg seed. Fixes atmospheric N.
- **Azotobacter / Azospirillum**: for non-legumes (cereals, cotton, vegetables). Seed treatment or soil application.
- **PSB (Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria)**: releases locked-up P in soil. Use with DAP → get more from less fertilizer.
- **KSB (Potash Solubilising Bacteria)**: similar for K.
- **Mycorrhiza (AMF)**: fungal symbiont — boosts root P/Zn uptake, drought resistance.

## Bio-Pesticides
- **Trichoderma viride / harzianum**: seed treatment 4 g/kg; soil drench for damping-off / wilt / root rot.
- **Pseudomonas fluorescens**: against rice blast, soil-borne pathogens.
- **Beauveria bassiana**: fungal insecticide — whitefly, mealybug, thrips.
- **Metarhizium anisopliae**: white grub, termite.
- **HaNPV / SpNPV**: Helicoverpa / Spodoptera nuclear polyhedrosis virus — species-specific biocontrol. 250 LE/acre.
- **Trichogramma chilonis / pretiosum**: egg parasitoid — release 50k/acre × 4–6 times for stem borer, fall armyworm, bollworm.
- **Neem**: 3–5 ml/L foliar. Neem cake 200 kg/acre as soil amendment.

## Vermicomposting
- **Worms**: Eisenia fetida, Eudrilus eugeniae.
- **Setup**: 3x2x1 ft pit/tank, cow dung + shredded organic waste + neem leaves (keeps ants away).
- **Output**: 1 kg worms → 100 kg vermicompost in 60 days.
- **Apply**: 2 t/acre (field) or 200 g/plant (vegetables).

# Market & Price Intelligence

## MSP (Minimum Support Price)
- Announced by CACP for **23 notified crops**:
  - 7 cereals (paddy, wheat, maize, bajra, jowar, ragi, barley)
  - 5 pulses (chana, tur, urad, moong, masoor)
  - 7 oilseeds (mustard, groundnut, soybean, sunflower, sesame, safflower, niger)
  - 4 commercial (cotton, sugarcane FRP, jute, copra)
- MSP changes each season — check cacp.dacnet.nic.in or pib.gov.in.
- MSP applies IF govt procurement agency (FCI, CCI, NAFED, state procurement) is active in your area.

## Mandi & Price Check
- **AgMarkNet** (agmarknet.gov.in): daily arrivals + prices from 2700+ mandis across India. Has free API.
- **e-NAM** (enam.gov.in): unified online mandi — register via nearest APMC, better price discovery.
- **State AGMARKNET apps**: each state also has its own.
- Always check **multiple mandis** before selling — prices vary 5–20% across nearby markets.

## FPOs (Farmer Producer Organisations)
- **10,000 FPO scheme** (Centre): ₹18 lakh support + 5-year management cost + credit guarantee.
- **Benefits**: bulk input purchase (cheaper seeds/fertiliser/pesticides), direct market access (bypass middlemen), collective bargaining, processing/branding.
- **How to join**: check NABARD / SFAC / NCDC lists for FPOs near you; or start one (min 300 members typically).

## Contract Farming (APLM Act, 2017 model)
- Advantages: assured buyer, assured price, input support.
- Risks: agreement lock-in, buyer default.
- ALWAYS: written contract, dispute resolution clause, check buyer reputation, don't lock in whole farm.
- State implementation varies — check state agri produce and livestock marketing Act.

## Storage & Post-Harvest
- **Losses in India**: 5–15% for grains, 20–30% for fruits/vegetables — HUGE opportunity to save.
- **Options**:
  - Warehouse Receipt System: store in WDRA-registered warehouse, get receipt → can use as collateral for pledge loan at 7%.
  - **Cold storage**: for potato, onion, fruits. Subsidy under MIDH, AIF.
  - **Hermetic bags (PICS)**: zero-oxygen, kills pests without chemicals. Good for small quantities of pulses/grains.
- **Scientific storage** = grade + clean + dry to safe moisture (paddy 13%, wheat 12%, pulses 10%) → store in cool, dry, rodent-proof space.

## Value Addition (higher income)
- **Primary processing**: cleaning, grading, drying, packing.
- **Secondary**: milling, oil extraction, turmeric powder, flour, juice, pickle, papad.
- **PMFME scheme**: 35% subsidy up to ₹10 lakh for micro food processing. Ideal for FPOs / SHGs.
- **ODOP (One District One Product)** under PMFME: scheme targets one key product per district.

## Digital Platforms for Farmers
- **Kisan Suvidha** (GoI app): weather, prices, advisory, schemes.
- **eNAM** (app + web): online mandi trading.
- **Pusa Krishi** (ICAR): research-based advisory.
- **Plantix, CropIn, AgroStar, BigHaat**: private apps for crop disease ID + input procurement.
- **mKisan SMS Portal**: free SMS advisory (register at mkisan.gov.in).
- **Kisan Call Centre**: 1800-180-1551 (toll-free, 22 local languages).

## Credit — Smart Use
- KCC up to ₹3 lakh at effective 4%. USE it for inputs, not consumption.
- PM-KUSUM solar pump loan: often cheaper than diesel long-term.
- **AVOID**: informal moneylender loans at 24–60%.
- FPO membership often unlocks easier credit.

## Insurance Beyond PMFBY
- **Weather-Based Crop Insurance (WBCIS)**: for weather-dependent crops not fully covered by PMFBY.
- **Cattle insurance**: for dairy animals; premium partly subsidised.
- **Accident insurance** for farmer families via PMSBY (₹20/year).

## Marketing Tips for Farmers
1. Grade your produce — graded sells 10–15% higher.
2. Clean + dry properly — reduces buyer rejection.
3. Track 3 mandis before deciding — go where price is best + cost of transport justifies.
4. Join an FPO — even as an ordinary member, input prices drop.
5. Build repeat-buyer relationships — loyalty often beats spot-market gains.
6. Consider direct-to-consumer (farmers' markets, weekly haats, online via ONDC pilot) for vegetables/fruits.
