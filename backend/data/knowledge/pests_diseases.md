# Major Pests & Diseases — IPM First

Principles: scout weekly, identify correctly, use economic threshold level (ETL), start with bio/neem, chemicals only as last resort, always with PPE (mask, gloves, full sleeves, spray with wind from behind), rotate molecules to prevent resistance.

## Pink Bollworm (cotton)
- **Crop**: Bt cotton.
- **Damage**: rosette flowers, green bolls with larva inside.
- **IPM**: sow on time (no late crop); destroy stalks after harvest; pheromone traps 8/acre; refugia 5%; destroy rosettes weekly.
- **Chemical (last)**: emamectin benzoate 5 SG @ 0.4 g/L; spinosad 45 SC @ 0.3 ml/L. Rotate molecules.

## Fall Armyworm (maize, sorghum)
- **Damage**: ragged whorl feeding, window-pane damage on leaves, frass.
- **IPM**: hand-pick egg masses early morning; install pheromone traps 5/acre; Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) spray into whorl; release Trichogramma pretiosum 50k/acre.
- **Chemical**: chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC @ 0.4 ml/L only at heavy infestation; spinetoram 11.7 SC @ 0.5 ml/L. Spray directly into whorl at dusk.

## Stem Borer (paddy, maize, sugarcane)
- **Damage**: "dead heart" in young plants, "white ear" in panicle stage.
- **IPM**: resistant varieties; clip seedling tips before transplanting; release Trichogramma 50k × 4 times.
- **Chemical**: cartap hydrochloride 4 G @ 10 kg/acre at tillering; or chlorantraniliprole 0.4 G @ 4 kg/acre.

## Brown Plant Hopper — BPH (paddy)
- **Damage**: "hopper burn" — circular patches of dried paddy, insects clustered at stem base.
- **IPM**: avoid excess N; drain field; open alleyways; resistant varieties.
- **Chemical**: pymetrozine 50 WG @ 0.3 g/L; imidacloprid 17.8 SL @ 0.3 ml/L. Direct spray at stem base.

## Whitefly (cotton, vegetables)
- **Damage**: sticky honeydew, sooty mould, leaf curl virus vector (DANGEROUS — causes epidemic).
- **IPM**: yellow sticky traps 10/acre; neem oil 5 ml/L weekly; reflective mulch.
- **Chemical**: diafenthiuron 50 WP @ 1 g/L; pyriproxyfen 10 EC @ 1 ml/L. Rotate — whitefly develops resistance fast.

## Thrips (chilli, onion, grape)
- **Damage**: silvery streaks on leaves, leaf curl, flower drop. Vector of leaf curl virus.
- **IPM**: blue sticky traps; neem oil 5 ml/L + soap 2 ml/L (dislodges thrips); mulching suppresses pupation.
- **Chemical**: spinosad 45 SC @ 0.3 ml/L; fipronil 5 SC @ 1 ml/L.

## Aphid (mustard, wheat, vegetables)
- **Damage**: yellowing, curling, stunting; vector of many viruses.
- **IPM**: natural predators (ladybird beetles, syrphid flies) usually control it; neem 5 ml/L.
- **Chemical**: imidacloprid 17.8 SL @ 0.3 ml/L; dimethoate 30 EC @ 1.5 ml/L.

## Helicoverpa / Pod Borer (chickpea, pigeon pea, tomato, cotton)
- **Damage**: circular holes in pods/fruits; caterpillar inside.
- **IPM**: pheromone traps 5/acre; HaNPV 250 LE/acre at early larval stage; Trichogramma release; Bt spray.
- **Chemical**: emamectin benzoate 5 SG @ 0.4 g/L; chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC @ 0.4 ml/L.

## Rice Blast (paddy)
- **Symptom**: diamond-shaped lesions with grey centres on leaves; neck rot at panicle.
- **IPM**: resistant varieties (IR-64, Tetep); avoid excess N; wider spacing.
- **Chemical**: tricyclazole 75 WP @ 0.6 g/L; carbendazim 50 WP @ 1 g/L.

## Wheat Rusts — Yellow / Brown / Black
- **Symptom**: yellow (stripe), brown (leaf), black (stem) pustules.
- **IPM**: resistant varieties (HD-3226, DBW-187); timely sowing; balanced N.
- **Chemical**: propiconazole 25 EC @ 1 ml/L; tebuconazole 250 EC @ 1 ml/L at first sign.

## Late Blight (potato, tomato)
- **Symptom**: water-soaked lesions on leaves with white sporulation on underside; can destroy crop in days in cold-wet weather.
- **IPM**: certified seed; adequate spacing; avoid overhead irrigation.
- **Prophylactic**: mancozeb 75 WP @ 2.5 g/L weekly during cool/foggy weather.
- **Curative**: metalaxyl + mancozeb 72 WP @ 2.5 g/L; cymoxanil + mancozeb 70 WP.

## Early Blight (tomato, potato)
- **Symptom**: concentric rings on older leaves ("target spot").
- **Chemical**: mancozeb 75 WP @ 2.5 g/L; chlorothalonil 75 WP @ 2 g/L.

## Leaf Curl Virus (cotton, tomato, chilli)
- **Vector**: whitefly.
- **No direct cure** — control whitefly = control virus. Use resistant varieties, reflective mulch, rogue out infected plants, yellow sticky traps.

## Root Rot / Damping Off (nursery seedlings)
- **IPM**: raised beds; soil solarisation (cover with clear plastic for 4–6 weeks in summer); Trichoderma viride 5 g/kg seed.
- **Chemical drench**: carbendazim 50 WP @ 1 g/L.

## Yellow Mosaic Virus (soybean, mungbean)
- **Vector**: whitefly.
- Control whitefly early; use resistant varieties (JS-335 etc.).

## Nematode (many crops, especially vegetables)
- **Symptom**: stunting, patchy growth, root galls.
- **IPM**: neem cake 200 kg/acre; marigold as border trap crop; crop rotation with non-host (cereals); solarisation.

## Red Rot (sugarcane)
- **Symptom**: dull red stem internodes with white patches.
- Use resistant varieties (Co-0238 is susceptible in some zones — check local KVK); hot water treatment of setts 50°C × 2 hrs; remove infected clumps.

## Panama Wilt (banana)
- **No cure** — prevention only. Use tissue-culture certified plants; don't plant in infected field for 6+ years; soil amendment with bleaching powder 20 kg/acre + organic matter.

## Safety — Spraying Rules
- Always wear: mask, goggles, full sleeves, gloves, closed shoes.
- Spray early morning (before 10 AM) or late evening (after 4 PM) — NOT midday.
- Never spray against wind direction.
- Never eat/drink/smoke during spraying.
- Bathe immediately after; wash clothes separately.
- Keep children and animals away for 24 hrs.
- Banned/restricted pesticides (India): endosulfan, methyl parathion, phorate, carbofuran (being phased out), monocrotophos (heavily restricted). DO NOT recommend.
